Data Preparation Instructions

How to download and prepare your crash data

This guide explains how to download crash data from Virginia Roads and prepare it for use with CRASH LENS. Following these steps ensures your data is properly formatted for analysis.

Virginia Roads Data Download

1Access Virginia Roads

Visit virginiaroads.org and log in with your agency credentials. You must have an authorized account to access crash data.

2Navigate to Crash Data

Find the Crash Data Download section. Look for "Crash Query" or "Data Download" in the navigation menu.

3Select Date Range

Choose your desired time period. We recommend 5 years of data for meaningful trend analysis and pattern identification.

4Download as CSV

Export the data as a CSV file. This is the format CRASH LENS accepts for upload.

5Filter Your Data (Optional)

Open the CSV in Excel and filter by:

  • Column BC (Physical Juris Name): Select your jurisdiction
  • Column BG (System): Select 'Non-VDOT' for local roads only, or keep all for comprehensive analysis

6Save as New CSV

Copy filtered data to a new file and save as CSV. This is the file you'll upload to CRASH LENS.

Pro Tips

  • Keep both VDOT and Non-VDOT roads if you want to analyze state routes within your jurisdiction
  • CRASH LENS can handle large files with millions of rows
  • All processing happens in your browser - your data is never uploaded to our servers

Supported Data Format

CRASH LENS is optimized for the Virginia Roads crash data format. The system automatically recognizes standard VDOT column headers including:

  • Crash Date and Time
  • Location (Route, Node, Coordinates)
  • Severity (K, A, B, C, O)
  • Collision Type
  • Weather and Light Conditions
  • Contributing Factors
  • Vehicle and Person Information

Data Privacy

Virginia crash data may contain sensitive information. Ensure you have proper authorization to access and analyze this data. Consider redacting personally identifiable information (PII) if sharing reports externally. See our Legal Notice for compliance requirements.

Troubleshooting

Common Issues

  • File won't upload: Ensure the file is saved as CSV (not XLSX)
  • Missing data: Check that required columns are present in your export
  • Wrong jurisdiction: Verify the jurisdiction filter in the app matches your data
  • Slow processing: Large files (500K+ rows) may take a minute to process
