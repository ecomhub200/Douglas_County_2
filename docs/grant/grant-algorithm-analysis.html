Grant-Ready Locations Algorithm: Improvement Analysis

From arbitrary weights to statistically-validated, evidence-based scoring

1 Current Algorithm Weaknesses

10 Critical Issues Identified

#ProblemImpactExample
1 No Statistical Significance Testing High 2/3 crashes impaired (67%) triggers 405d, but could be random noise. No binomial test.
2 No Exposure Normalization High High-ADT roads always rank higher even when their rate is safe. Volume ≠ danger.
3 No Expected vs Observed Comparison High SPORTS/HSM core method: PSI = Observed − Expected. Missing entirely.
4 No Regression to Mean Correction Medium Random spike years inflate rankings. Empirical Bayes prevents this.
5 Arbitrary Weight Selection Medium vruPed=40 vs vruBike=35 — no empirical basis. Changes rankings dramatically.
6 No B/C Ratio Estimation High HSIP scores 35% on B/C ratio. Algorithm never estimates it.
7 No Equity Analysis Medium SS4A scores 20% on equity. No Census/CEJST overlay.
8 Arbitrary Pattern Thresholds Medium "impaired > 25%" triggers 405d — why 25%? Should compare to county baseline.
9 No Countermeasure Feasibility Check Medium Identifies problems but doesn't check if proven countermeasures (with CMFs) exist.
10 Grant Matching Too Broad Medium Almost every location matches HSIP (any K or A). Matching loses discriminative power.

3 Key Improvements Explained

❌ Current: Arbitrary Thresholds

if (impairedPct > 25%) → 405d if (speedPct > 35%) → 402 if (vru > 0 && K+A > 0) → SS4A

Hard-coded percentages with no statistical basis. A location with 2/4 impaired (50%) scores high even though this could easily be random.

✅ Improved: Statistical Evidence

ORI = location_rate / county_rate p-value = binomial_test(observed, n, baseline) if (p < 0.10 && ORI > 1.5) → significant

Compare each location to the county average using Over-Representation Index. Only flag patterns that are statistically significant — not random noise.

❌ Current: Additive Weighted Score

score = K^1.5 × 150 + A × 60 + ped × 40 + angle × 10 + speed × 15 ...

Single score from arbitrary weights. Why is ped=40 and bike=35? Small weight changes cause dramatic re-ranking.

✅ Improved: Multi-Dimensional Scoring

needScore = f(PSI, severity, volume) patternScore = f(ORI, significance, sample_size) feasibility = f(B/C ratio, CMF availability) grantFit = f(actual NOFO criteria weights)

Four independent sub-scores, each with clear methodology. Engineers can see WHY a location ranks high and which dimension drives it.

❌ Current: No Expected Crash Comparison

// Just counts crashes score += total × weight // High-volume roads always win

A road with 100K ADT and 50 crashes ranks above a road with 5K ADT and 20 crashes, even though the second is far more dangerous per vehicle.

✅ Improved: PSI (SPORTS-Aligned)

expected = county_avg × ADT_adjustment EB_estimate = w × expected + (1-w) × observed PSI = EB_estimate - expected Locations with highest EXCESS rank first

Empirical Bayes methodology used by VDOT SPORTS. Identifies locations with genuinely more crashes than expected — not just high volume.

NHTSA 402 & 405d

ProgramKey DifferentiatorStatistical Test
402 (Behavioral)Speed/distracted ORI > 1.5 AND p < 0.10Binomial test vs county speed rate
405d (Impaired)Impaired ORI > 2.0 AND p < 0.10Binomial test vs county impaired rate + weekend-night correlation

Both require statistically significant behavioral patterns — not just raw counts above arbitrary thresholds.

6 Confidence System (New)

ConfidenceCriteriaWhat It Means
HIGH ≥15 crashes AND ≥2 significant patterns Strong statistical evidence. Findings are robust and grant-ready.
MEDIUM ≥8 crashes AND ≥1 significant pattern Reasonable evidence. Patterns likely real but could benefit from more data.
LOW Below medium thresholds Limited evidence. Patterns may be noise. Use caution in grant applications.

What Stays the Same

The UI structure, table layout, tier coloring, pagination, date filters, scoring profile dropdown, and ADT toggle all remain identical. The improvement is entirely in the scoring engine underneath.

What Changes

Rankings become evidence-based. The score column now reflects genuine safety need + grant fit instead of arbitrary weighted sums. The "Best Match" column uses statistical evidence instead of hard-coded thresholds.
