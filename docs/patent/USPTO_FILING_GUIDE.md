## STEP 1: CREATE USPTO ACCOUNT

### 1.1 Register at USPTO

1. Go to: https://www.uspto.gov/
2. Click "Patents" → "Apply Online"
3. Click "Create Account" at MyUSPTO
4. Provide:
   - Email address
   - Create password
   - Security questions
5. Verify email and complete registration

### 1.2 Set Up Patent Center Access

1. Go to: https://patentcenter.uspto.gov/
2. Log in with your MyUSPTO credentials
3. Complete any additional verification required
4. Bookmark this page - you'll file here

## STEP 2: PREPARE DOCUMENTS

### 2.1 Required Documents

| Document | File Name | Format |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Specification | `specification.pdf` | PDF |
| Claims (optional but recommended) | Included in specification | PDF |
| Drawings/Figures | `drawings.pdf` | PDF |
| Cover Sheet | Auto-generated | Online form |

### 2.2 Convert Your Documents

**From this repository, prepare:**

1. **Specification Document**
   - Source: `docs/patent/PROVISIONAL_PATENT_SPECIFICATION.md`
   - Convert to PDF
   - Fill in your personal information
   - Sign where indicated

2. **Figures Document**
   - Source: `docs/patent/FIGURES.md`
   - Convert ASCII diagrams to clean drawings (optional but recommended)
   - Or submit as-is with ASCII art

3. **Invention Disclosure** (keep for your records)
   - Source: `docs/patent/INVENTION_DISCLOSURE.md`
   - Not submitted to USPTO
   - Keep dated, signed copy for your records

### 2.3 Document Formatting Requirements

**Specification:**
- Page size: 8.5" x 11" (US Letter) or A4
- Margins: At least 1" on all sides
- Font: Times New Roman, Arial, or Courier
- Font size: 12 point minimum
- Line spacing: 1.5 or double
- Page numbers: Required

**Drawings:**
- Black ink on white background
- No photographs (unless unavoidable)
- Reference numerals must match specification
- Clear enough to photocopy

### 3.4 Claim Micro Entity Status

**Requirements for Micro Entity Status:**

You qualify as a micro entity if ALL of the following are true:

- [ ] You qualify as a small entity (individual, not assigned to large company)
- [ ] You have NOT been named as inventor on more than 4 previously filed patent applications
- [ ] Your gross income in the prior year was less than 3× the median household income (~$229,908 in 2024)
- [ ] You have NOT assigned/licensed rights to an entity that exceeds the income limit

**During filing:**
1. Select "Micro Entity" when asked about entity status
2. Certify you meet all requirements (checkbox)

## STEP 5: NEXT STEPS (NON-PROVISIONAL)

### 5.1 Timeline

Your provisional application expires in **12 months**. Before then, you must:

1. File a non-provisional (utility) patent application, OR
2. File a PCT international application, OR
3. Abandon the application

### 5.2 Non-Provisional Costs (Micro Entity)

| Fee | Amount |
|-----|--------|
| Basic Filing Fee | $320 |
| Search Fee | $160 |
| Examination Fee | $200 |
| **Total Initial** | **$680** |
| Issue Fee (if approved) | $280 |
| Maintenance Fees (over 20 years) | ~$4,000 |

### 5.3 Consider Patent Attorney

For the non-provisional, consider hiring a patent attorney:
- More rigorous examination
- Office actions require legal responses
- Claims prosecution is complex
- Cost: $5,000-15,000 depending on complexity

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

### Q: Can I add more material after filing?

**A:** No. A provisional application cannot be amended. Any new material requires a new provisional application with a new priority date.

### Q: What happens if I miss the 12-month deadline?

**A:** The provisional application expires and cannot be renewed. You lose the priority date. You could file a new non-provisional, but it would have a later priority date.

### Q: Do I need formal drawings?

**A:** For a provisional, informal drawings (even hand-drawn) are acceptable. However, better drawings strengthen your application if it's later challenged.

### Q: Can I file internationally?

**A:** A provisional only provides priority in the US. For international protection, you must file a PCT application within 12 months claiming priority from your provisional.

### Q: What if someone copies my invention before the patent issues?

**A:** You cannot enforce patent rights until the patent issues. However, once issued, you may be entitled to damages back to the filing date in some cases.

### Q: Should I include the claims in my provisional?

**A:** Claims are optional for provisionals, but recommended. Including them:
- Clarifies what you're claiming
- Helps when drafting the non-provisional
- Strengthens the priority claim if challenged
