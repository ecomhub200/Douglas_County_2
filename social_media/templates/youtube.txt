YOUTUBE VIDEO CONTENT GUIDELINES:

FORMAT:
- Title: Maximum 100 characters (aim for 60 for full display)
- Description: Maximum 5,000 characters
- Tags: Up to 500 characters total
- Shorts: Under 60 seconds vertical video

TITLE FORMULA:
[Keyword] + [Benefit/Hook] + [Qualifier]
Examples:
- "Crash Data Analysis: How to Find Intersection Hotspots in Minutes"
- "Traffic Safety Tools Every DOT Engineer Needs in 2026"
- "5 Crash Countermeasures That Actually Work (Data Proof)"

DESCRIPTION STRUCTURE:
[2-3 sentence summary with keywords]

[Timestamps]
0:00 - Introduction
1:30 - [Section 1]
3:00 - [Section 2]
...

[Links and resources]
Try CRASH LENS free: [website]
Related videos: [links]

[About CRASH LENS paragraph]

[Hashtags -- 3-5 max]

YOUTUBE-SPECIFIC TIPS:
- First 48 hours are critical for algorithm performance
- Thumbnail is the #1 factor for click-through rate
- Titles should include searchable keywords
- Ask viewers to comment with specific questions
- Pin a comment with a CTA or summary
- Shorts (<60s) grow subscribers faster than long-form
- Chapters (timestamps) improve watch time and SEO

VIDEO CONTENT IDEAS:
1. Product Tutorials: "How to Use CRASH LENS CMF Analysis"
2. Data Walkthroughs: "Analyzing 5 Years of Crash Data in Douglas County"
3. Concept Explainers: "What is EPDO Scoring and Why It Matters"
4. Industry Updates: "New FHWA Safety Guidelines: What You Need to Know"
5. Shorts: "One stat that will change how you think about intersection safety"

ACCESSIBILITY REQUIREMENTS:
- All videos MUST have closed captions/subtitles
- Use clear, high-contrast text overlays
- Describe visual elements in narration
- Provide transcript in description or comments

DO NOT:
- Use clickbait titles that don't match content
- Neglect captions/subtitles
- Make videos longer than necessary
- Forget to include keywords in title and description
