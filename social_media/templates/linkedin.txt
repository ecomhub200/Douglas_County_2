LINKEDIN POST GUIDELINES:

FORMAT:
- Start with a strong hook line (question, surprising stat, or bold statement)
- Use short paragraphs (1-3 sentences each)
- Add line breaks between paragraphs for readability
- Use bullet points or numbered lists for data
- End with a question or CTA to drive engagement
- Keep under 1300 characters for optimal engagement (full limit: 3000)

TONE:
- Professional and authoritative
- Data-driven with specific numbers
- Thought leadership positioning
- Address the reader directly ("As a traffic engineer, you know...")
- Share insights, not just announcements

STRUCTURE TEMPLATE:
[Hook: Surprising stat or question]

[Context: 2-3 sentences explaining the significance]

[Key points: Bullet points with data]
- Point 1
- Point 2
- Point 3

[Insight: Your unique perspective or analysis]

[CTA: Question to drive comments, or link to learn more]

LINKEDIN-SPECIFIC TIPS:
- Posts with images get 2x engagement
- Posts asking questions get 50% more comments
- Tag relevant organizations (@FHWA, @AASHTO, @NHTSA) when applicable
- Use line breaks generously -- LinkedIn's feed compresses long paragraphs
- First 2-3 lines are visible before "see more" -- make them count
- Optimal length: 1,000-1,300 characters
- Best posting times: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM EST

DO NOT:
- Use excessive emojis
- Write in a salesy or promotional tone
- Start with "I'm excited to announce..." or similar cliches
- Use AI-sounding phrases like "In today's fast-paced world..."
