TIKTOK POST GUIDELINES:

FORMAT:
- Video: 15-60 seconds optimal (up to 10 minutes allowed)
- Caption: Maximum 2,200 characters
- Vertical format (9:16 aspect ratio)
- Text overlays for key points

TONE:
- Educational but entertaining ("edutainment")
- Surprising and hook-driven
- Conversational, not corporate
- Use trending sounds/formats when relevant to safety topics

HOOK FORMULA (First 3 Seconds):
- "Did you know [surprising stat]?"
- "This intersection has [X] crashes per year. Here's why."
- "Traffic engineers don't want you to know this..."
- "The most dangerous [road type/time/condition] is..."
- "I analyzed [X] crashes and found something shocking"

CAPTION STRUCTURE:
[Short, punchy summary of the video content]

[1-2 sentences of additional context]

[CTA: "Follow for more safety data" or "Comment your city"]

[3-5 hashtags]

TIKTOK-SPECIFIC TIPS:
- First 3 seconds determine if viewers stay
- Educational content performs exceptionally well
- Use text overlays -- many watch without sound
- Trending sounds increase discoverability
- Series content ("Part 1 of X") builds followers
- Duet/stitch with relevant content for reach
- Post consistently (same time, same days)

VIDEO CONTENT IDEAS:
1. "Did you know?" safety facts with data visualization
2. Before/after of intersection improvements
3. "Guess the crash cause" interactive content
4. Quick tool demos (30-second CRASH LENS feature showcase)
5. Day-in-the-life of a traffic safety analyst
6. Crash data trends set to trending audio
7. "Rate this intersection" safety assessment

ACCESSIBILITY:
- Always include captions/text overlays
- Use high contrast text
- Avoid flashing content
- Provide audio descriptions for visual-only content

DO NOT:
- Be overly corporate or salesy
- Ignore trends -- adapt them to your niche
- Make videos too long (aim for 30-60 seconds)
- Use insensitive language about crash victims
