INSTAGRAM POST GUIDELINES:

FORMAT:
- Maximum 2,200 characters for captions
- Up to 30 hashtags (use 10-15 for optimal reach)
- First line is the hook (visible before "more")
- Use line breaks and spacing for readability
- Reels: 15-90 seconds for maximum reach

TONE:
- Visual-first storytelling
- Educational but engaging
- Use analogies to make technical concepts accessible
- Inspire action and awareness

CAPTION STRUCTURE:
[Hook line -- visible before "more" tap]

[Story or insight -- 3-5 short sentences]

[Data point or statistic]

[CTA: "Save this post", "Tag a colleague", "Comment below"]

.
.
.
[Hashtag block -- separated by dots/line breaks from main text]

INSTAGRAM-SPECIFIC TIPS:
- Carousel posts (swipeable images) get highest engagement
- Reels reach 2x more non-followers than static posts
- Save-worthy content (tips, data, checklists) gets boosted by algorithm
- Use Stories for behind-the-scenes and polls
- Post consistently at the same times
- Use location tags for local visibility
- Alt text is REQUIRED for accessibility

VISUAL CONTENT IDEAS:
- Data visualization infographics (crash trends, hotspot maps)
- Before/after comparisons of safety improvements
- "Did you know?" stat cards
- Step-by-step carousel tutorials
- Short demo reels of CRASH LENS features
- Quote graphics from safety leaders

IMAGE ALT TEXT REQUIREMENTS (WCAG 2.1 AA):
- Every image MUST have descriptive alt text
- Describe what the image shows, not just "infographic"
- Include key data points mentioned in the image
- Example: "Bar chart showing crash severity distribution: 45% property damage only, 30% possible injury, 15% minor injury, 8% serious injury, 2% fatal"

DO NOT:
- Post without an image (Instagram is visual-first)
- Use irrelevant hashtags for reach
- Neglect alt text
- Post only product promotions
